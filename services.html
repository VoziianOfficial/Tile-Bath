Before hiring

What homeowners should compare directly with providers.

Tile & Bath can help create the connection, but the final hiring decision belongs to the homeowner. Review the details carefully before moving forward.

License and insurance

Ask each provider what credentials apply in your state, city, and project type.

Written estimate scope

Compare labor, materials, exclusions, disposal, payment terms, and warranty details in writing.

Measurement process

Confirm how measurements are taken, who verifies them, and what happens if the opening needs adjustment.

Timeline and access

Ask about lead times, project sequencing, home access, cleanup, and expected disruption.

Comparison mindset

A polished bathroom remodeling project starts with clearer questions.

Whether the project is practical, design-led, energy-focused, or repair-based, use the provider conversation to clarify product fit, installation approach, communication style, and long-term responsibility.

Product fit Material package, waterproofing scope, fixture style, layout fit, and home conditions.
Provider fit Availability, service area, communication, documentation, and warranty terms.
Project fit Budget range, timing, access, permits, cleanup, and final contract scope.
